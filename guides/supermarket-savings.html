Supermarket Savings

Continente vs Pingo Doce vs Lidl vs Aldi — where to genuinely save money, how the loyalty cards work, and how to stack discounts like a local.

Last updated: April 2025 Free guide 8 min read

The Portuguese supermarket landscape

Portugal's grocery market is dominated by a small number of players, each with a distinct positioning. Continente and Pingo Doce are the two established Portuguese chains and together account for the majority of the market. Lidl and Aldi have made significant inroads over the past decade, winning on price for dry goods and own-brand products. Understanding how each chain works — and when to shop at which — is the key to cutting your grocery bill without reducing what you eat.

Unlike the UK or Ireland, where switching supermarkets entirely is common, most Portuguese households use two or three chains in combination: a big weekly shop at one, fresh produce at another on specific days, and periodic Lidl or Aldi runs for pantry staples. If you adopt the same habit, you can realistically save €50–100 per month on a typical family shop without changing what you buy.

Continente — the loyalty card explained

The Cartão Continente is one of the most generous loyalty schemes in Portugal and arguably the best reason to make Continente your primary supermarket. The card is free to obtain in-store or online and immediately unlocks a tier of personalised discounts that are not available to non-cardholders. Each week, the Continente app generates a list of "ofertas personalizadas" — personalised offers on products you actually buy — that can save 20–50% on individual items.

Beyond the weekly personalised offers, the Cartão Continente also accumulates points on every purchase. These points translate into vouchers (cheques Continente) that can be spent in-store, at Continente petrol stations, or at a range of partner businesses including Wells pharmacies, Note! stationery shops, and the ZAP broadband service. Key benefits of the Continente card include:

  • Personalised weekly discounts on items you regularly buy
  • Points on every purchase, redeemable as vouchers in-store and with partners
  • Access to exclusive member pricing on selected product categories
  • Family card sharing — up to five family members can share the same card balance

Pingo Doce — how the weekly deals work

Visit Pingo Doce →

Pingo Doce operates differently to Continente. Rather than a points-based loyalty programme, Pingo Doce runs a weekly rotation of deep discounts across a selection of products — particularly strong on fresh produce, meat, fish and prepared meals. The deals typically reset on Wednesdays, making Wednesday the best day to shop at Pingo Doce. The Poupa Mais card accumulates points that convert to vouchers, but the real value is in the weekly deals rather than the card itself.

Pingo Doce's prepared food counters — rotisserie chicken, fresh salads, hot meals — are widely considered the best value ready-to-eat food in Portuguese supermarkets. The quality is consistently high and the price is competitive with fast food. If you're shopping for a quick weeknight dinner rather than a full week's groceries, Pingo Doce often wins on the fresh and prepared food front.

Lidl and Aldi — when they win on price

For dry goods, cleaning products, own-brand staples and certain fresh produce categories, Lidl and Aldi are consistently the cheapest option in Portugal. The Lidl Portugal circular runs weekly and often includes short-run promotions on non-grocery items — kitchenware, tools, seasonal clothing — at prices that are difficult to beat. Neither Lidl nor Aldi operates a points-based loyalty scheme in Portugal, so what you see is what you get: straightforward low prices with no hoops to jump through. The trade-off is a narrower range of products and less consistency in what's available week to week.

How to stack your savings

Pro tip: Use your Continente card for the bulk shop, then Pingo Doce on Wednesdays for fresh produce when their weekly deals reset. Add a monthly Lidl or Aldi run for dry goods and cleaning products and you'll cover all three price tiers with minimal effort.

The most effective strategy combines the Continente personalised discount system with Pingo Doce's Wednesday fresh produce deals and periodic Lidl or Aldi runs for pantry staples. The key is to check the Continente app before each shop — if an item you need is on your personalised offers list, buy it at Continente. If not, check whether it's on the Pingo Doce weekly list. For categories where neither chain has a deal, Lidl is usually the default cheapest option.

One underused tactic is to register your NIF at the checkout of every supermarket — even Lidl and Aldi. While these chains don't have loyalty programmes, linking purchases to your NIF counts toward your IRS household deductions (despesas gerais familiares), which can reduce your annual tax bill by up to €250.

The apps worth downloading

  • Continente app — access personalised offers, Cartão Continente balance and online ordering
  • Pingo Doce app — view weekly deals before you shop and manage your Poupa Mais card
  • Lidl Plus — Lidl's loyalty app, available in Portugal, with weekly digital coupons and scratch cards
  • Auchan — worth checking for electronics, large appliances and bulk household goods, where their prices are often competitive
