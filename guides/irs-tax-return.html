IRS Tax Return

Last updated: Free guide 15 min read
Independent · Always Free · Written for Residents · No Paid Placements · English Guides for Portugal
5 things to know before you start
  • Portugal's IRS is personal income tax — nothing to do with the US tax authority
  • The filing window runs from 1 April to 30 June every year
  • You file online only, at Portal das Finanças (portaldasfinancas.gov.pt)
  • If you owe tax, payment is due by 31 August
  • Most residents must file — even if tax was withheld from your salary

Do you need to file?

Did you spend 183 days or more in Portugal during this tax year?

Yes

You are a tax resident. You must file an IRS return and declare your worldwide income.

No — check the next question

Spending fewer than 183 days here does not automatically make you a non-resident. Continue below.

Did you have a permanent, habitual home in Portugal on 31 December — a place set up and available to you as your main residence?

Yes

You are a tax resident, regardless of days spent. Worldwide income must be declared in Portugal.

No

You are likely a non-resident for tax purposes. You declare only Portuguese-source income, taxed at a flat rate.
