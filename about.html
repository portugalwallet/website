The Portugal Wallet exists because most financial information in Portugal is either in Portuguese, buried in legal language, or written for wealthy expats with advisers.

Who we're for

We're for everyone else — the settled residents, the mixed families, the people who've been here long enough to have bills, bank accounts and tax returns, but still find the system hard to navigate in English. We cover the things that affect everyday life: energy tariffs, car insurance, supermarket loyalty cards, IRS filing, mortgages, Segurança Social and more.

How we work

Every guide is free, editorially independent, and written the way a knowledgeable friend would explain it — clearly, honestly, and without trying to sell you anything.

We research each topic from scratch, using official Portuguese sources, talking to people who have been through the process, and checking the guides regularly to make sure they stay accurate as systems and prices change. We write for someone who is intelligent and capable but doesn't speak Portuguese fluently and isn't familiar with how the Portuguese financial and administrative system works.

Our independence policy

The Portugal Wallet is editorially independent. Companies cannot pay to appear in our guides — we write about what's genuinely best for our readers, not what pays us best.

Some of our guides contain affiliate links — these are links to third-party tools and services. If you click one and sign up, we may earn a small commission. This never affects our editorial judgement: we only link to tools we would recommend regardless, and the deal you get is always identical to or better than going direct.

Every page that contains affiliate links says so clearly.

Who writes The Portugal Wallet

The Portugal Wallet is written and maintained by a small, independent team of English speakers who live and work in Portugal. We experience the same financial system you do — the same confusing bills, the same bureaucratic portals, the same language barrier. That's why we write these guides.
